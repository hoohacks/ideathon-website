F.A.Q

  • Student
  • Mentor
  • Sponsor
  • How many students are we aiming to have? How will acceptances work?

    We estimate around 75 students will show up. We will be releasing an application on the website being built for the event on January 5th and will close it on January 19th. We are planning on accepting everyone, but should the event become to large, we will select who we think will most benefit from it.

  • What should students be bringing to this event?

    Ideas, if they have them, if not, just a laptop.

  • Why should students come to this event? What will they get out of it?

    A mentor/point of contact and a team for their ideas. An understanding of what a hackathon is, experience in both COMM and CS and their conjunction. A market plan and a CS outline for building their application, free lunch, and potentially extra credit for their classes.

  • How many mentors are we trying to have?

    We estimate teams will be from 4-6 people, and would like for every team to be able to have at least one mentor. Mentors can stay for however long they would like and can take on however many teams they want. We would recommend that they not take more than 2 unless they would like more.

  • Where are we drawing mentors from?

    We are currently considering drawing mentors from Commerce faculty, CS professors and fourth-year students, and local VCâ€™s (contacted through VVF).

  • What is expected of mentors (including time commitment, additional prep, after the ideathon support)?

    We would like mentors to come in for, at minimum, an hour, but would love them to come for longer! We would also love to have mentors give talks or conduct workshops, if they are interested. We expect mentors to have 30-60 min meetings with each team they mentor and to maintain however much contact teams would like to have after the ideathon.

  • How are mentors assigned?

    We will not be assigning mentors. Instead, we will ask mentors to come up with a list of information they would need from teams to judge whether or not they would be a good mentor-mentee fit. Then, teams will fill out a form, and mentors can select the teams they feel they would be able to mentor well

  • What qualities are we looking for in sponsors?

    We are looking for groups or companies that develop or market new technology products.

  • What can sponsors get out of sponsoring us?

    Sponsors can acquaint themselves with UVA students who are passionate about a cool idea with the capability to execute on it. They can also develop relationships with UVA clubs, especially VVF and HooHacks. They can also help foster entrepreneurship at UVA.

  • What do we want out of sponsors?

    We would like sponsors to provide us with mentors and/or speakers to give talks/conduct workshops. We would also love it if our attendees could get to form connections with the sponsors for internships, or other learning opportunities.

  • What kind of students do we expect to have?

    We expect to have any student who is interested in entrepreneurship at UVA. We expect we will have a lot of:

    • McIntire Students - Entrepreneurial track, or IT concentration
    • CS Students - with entrepreneurial ideas, taking Advanced Software Development, Internet-Scale Applications, or other development classes
    • Pre-Commerce students looking to bolster their resumes for McIntire
    • Systems Engineers
